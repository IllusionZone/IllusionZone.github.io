【阅之载之】The Great Gastby

2023-10-27 阅之载之 694 字
阅之载之 book

阅读，总觉得必须做上一点什么，否则总有种空空荡荡的感觉。记下一些，写出一点，似乎就使得花费时间、花费经历读到的书更有质感一些，也许是心理安慰，也许是自欺欺人，总之且当聊以自慰。

主做摘录，偶发一点感想。

CHAPTER 1

In my younger and more vulnerable（脆弱的，易受伤的） years my father gave me some advice that I’ve been turning over（翻转，移动） in my mind ever since（从那时起）.

‘Whenever you feel like（感觉像是，想要，倾向于） criticizing（批判，批评） any one,’ he told me,‘just remember that all the people in this world haven’t had the advantages that you’ve had.’

He didn’t say any more but we’ve always been unusually（特别，极其，非常） communicative in a reserved（寡言少语的，内敛的，预定，保留） way, and I understood that he meant a great deal（大量，交易，坏运气，应对） more than that. In consequence（结果） I’m inclined（点头，倾向于，对…有兴趣，有天赋的） to reserve all judgments（褒贬，判断）, a habit that has opened up many curious（好奇的，求知欲强的，稀奇的，不寻常的） natures（性质） to me and also made me the victim（受害者，牺牲品） of not a few veteran（老兵，退伍军人，老练的） bores（钻孔，内径）. The abnormal mind is quick to detect and attach（连接，系上，依附） itself to this quality（质量，素质，品德） when it appears in a normal person, and so it came about that in college I was unjustly（不义地，不法地） accused（被告，指控，谴责） of being a politician（政客）, because I was privy（私人的，不公开的，有利害关系的人，厕所） to the secret griefs（悲伤，悲痛） of wild（野生的，狂暴的，野蛮的，自然地，不受控制地，偏远地区）, unknown men. Most of the confidences were unsought—frequently I have feigned sleep, preoccupation, or a hostile levity when I realized by some unmistakable sign that an intimate revelation was quivering on the horizon—for the intimate revelations of young men or at least the terms in which they express them are usually plagiaristic and marred by obvious suppressions. Reserving judgments is a matter of infnite hope. I am still a little afraid of missing something if I forget that, as my father snobbishly suggested, and I snobbishly repeat a sense of the fundamental decencies is parcelled out unequally at birth.

 译文 在我年轻而又脆弱的年纪，父亲为我提供了一些建议，自那时起，那些建议就一直反复地出现在我的脑海中。

“当你想要评判某人时”，他说道，“只要记住，你拥有着其他所有人都无法比拟的优势。”

他没有更深入地说下去，但我们总是以一种不同寻常的含蓄方式进行交流，我明白他的意思远不止于此。于是，我倾向于保留自己的一切判断，该习惯激发了我许多好奇的本性，也使我成为不少无聊人士的受害者。
